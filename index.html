WHAT IS QUANTUM COMPUTING?

CLASSICAL COMPUTING

  • Uses bits (0 or 1)
  • Sequential or parallel processing
  • Limited by Moore's Law
  • Approaching physical limits

QUANTUM COMPUTING

  • Uses qubits (0, 1, or both)
  • Exponential parallel processing
  • Leverages quantum mechanics
  • Solves complex problems faster

TASKS TAKING CENTURIES ON CLASSICAL COMPUTERS
CAN BE SOLVED IN MINUTES OR HOURS WITH QUANTUM COMPUTING

02

TECHNOLOGY DEEP DIVE

1. ENTANGLEMENT - BEYOND SPACE CONNECTION

Qubits can be linked through quantum entanglement, where the state of one qubit directly depends on another, regardless of distance.

  • A system of n qubits can represent 2^n STATES simultaneously
  • Combined with superposition, enables exponential parallel processing
  • Essential for molecular simulation, optimization, and quantum equation solving
  • Highly sensitive to decoherence - requires ULTRA-LOW TEMPERATURES (<15mK)

2. QUANTUM TUNNELING - ENERGY BARRIER BREAKTHROUGH

Allows quantum particles to penetrate energy barriers without classical energy requirements.

  • Foundation of superconducting qubits (used by Google, IBM, Rigetti)
  • Enables fast, stable, and energy-efficient state transitions
  • Creates quantized energy states through Josephson junctions
  • Dominant architecture for scalable quantum systems

3. MAJOR CHALLENGES

DECOHERENCE

Quantum states lose superposition when interacting with environment

  • Electromagnetic noise
  • Thermal fluctuations
  • Qubit correlation errors

CRYOGENIC ENVIRONMENT

Requires extreme cooling systems

  • ~15 millikelvin operation
  • Dilution refrigerators needed
  • Expensive and complex

CURRENT QUANTUM COMPUTING CAPABILITY

2019 - GOOGLE SYCAMORE

53 physical qubits achieved quantum supremacy: solved a specialized problem in ~200 seconds that would take classical supercomputers ~10,000 years

2023 - IBM CONDOR

1,121 physical qubits - but usable logical qubits remain low due to noise and decoherence

CURRENT STATE: NISQ ERA

Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum - devices with noise, unable to handle large-scale problems

WHY 20 MILLION QUBITS TO BREAK RSA-2048?

COMPONENT REQUIREMENT
Logical Qubits Needed ~4,000 (with error correction)
Physical Qubits per Logical Qubit ~5,000 (using surface code)
Total Physical Qubits ~20 MILLION
Time to Break RSA-2048 ~8 hours

GAP: 50 CURRENT QUBITS → 20 MILLION NEEDED
RSA encryption remains safe in the near term, but transition to post-quantum cryptography is underway

CLASSICAL VS QUANTUM COMPUTING EVOLUTION

MOORE'S LAW: THE CLASSICAL LIMIT

Transistor count doubles every 18-24 months, but approaching physical limits since mid-2010s

  • Electron leakage
  • Heat generation
  • Manufacturing costs
  • Approaching atomic-scale limits

QUANTUM GROWTH TRAJECTORY

Non-linear growth across multiple dimensions

ASPECT CLASSICAL COMPUTING QUANTUM COMPUTING
Growth Mechanism Predictable transistor increase Non-linear, multi-factor improvement
Current State Near physical saturation Rapidly increasing (qubits, fidelity, volume)
Hardware Innovation Speed Slowing down Accelerating across multiple fronts
Performance Advantage Stable, widespread applications Superior only for specialized problems

FROM SUPREMACY TO UTILITY

QUANTUM SUPREMACY (2019): Solving one specialized problem faster than classical computers

QUANTUM UTILITY (GOAL): Solving real-world problems like molecular simulation, optimization, RSA breaking

Requires: Millions of physical qubits + effective error correction + fault-tolerant algorithms + stable large-scale systems

BUSINESS CASE: VOLKSWAGEN

QUANTUM TRAFFIC OPTIMIZATION

PROJECT OVERVIEW

2017 - BEIJING PILOT

Analyzed GPS data from 10,000 taxis using quantum algorithms to optimize city-wide traffic flow

2019 - LISBON QUANTUM SHUTTLE

First live implementation at Web Summit - 9 shuttle buses with routes optimized every 2 minutes

BUSINESS IMPACT

  • REDUCED TRAVEL TIME: Dynamic routing minimized delays and improved punctuality
  • OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY: Better fleet distribution, reduced idle capacity
  • CONGESTION RELIEF: Distributed vehicles across multiple efficient paths
  • ENVIRONMENTAL BENEFITS: Smoother traffic flow → lower emissions
  • BRAND ENHANCEMENT: Positioned VW as quantum innovation leader

IMPLEMENTATION CHALLENGES

TECHNICAL LIMITATIONS

  • Limited qubits and connectivity
  • Required hybrid classical-quantum architecture
  • Real-time data quality issues
  • System integration complexity

BUSINESS CHALLENGES

  • High costs of quantum computing access
  • Difficulty scaling beyond pilot programs
  • Need for clear ROI demonstration
  • Unpredictable events (accidents, closures)

SUCCESS FACTORS

  • HYBRID STRATEGY: Combined classical and quantum to overcome hardware limits
  • SMALL-SCALE PILOTS: Controlled environment testing before expansion
  • CROSS-SECTOR COLLABORATION: D-Wave (hardware), Hexad (app), PTV Group (traffic modeling), CARRIS (operations)
  • CONTINUOUS OPTIMIZATION: 2-minute route recalculations, exclusion zones for narrow streets
  • LONG-TERM VISION: Integrated into broader smart mobility and MaaS strategy

REAL-WORLD APPLICATIONS

FINANCIAL SERVICES

CYBERSECURITY

Application: Protecting personal data from future quantum threats

Example: HSBC tested VPN channels with post-quantum algorithms and QRNG to secure cryptocurrency transactions

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Application: Enhancing predictive accuracy in complex data analysis

Example: QAmplifyNet predicts inventory backorders with higher accuracy than conventional ML. University of Chicago used QML to distinguish cancer patient exosomes from healthy individuals

ENERGY GRID MANAGEMENT

Application: Optimizing power generation and renewable energy distribution

Example: IonQ with Oak Ridge National Laboratory used 36-qubit quantum machine to solve "Unit Commitment" problem for power generation scheduling
